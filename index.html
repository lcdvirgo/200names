PALM OIL

That's thirty-six football fields of forest stripped from the world each minute. Unsustainable deforestation destroys natural biodiversity, poses severe social problems, and contributes 16% of all global greenhouse emissions.








The demand for palm oil is one of the biggest causes of deforestation because palm trees make money for farmers and for companies faster than resources from the rainforest.







Unsustainable deforestation causes many local issues, such as soil erosion. Increased flooding, lack of drinkable water, and inability to produce food causes many locals to work on plantations, worsening deforestation and at times subjecting them to inhumane working conditions.


The demand for palm oil is primarily driven from the west where it is found in products such as shampoo, ice cream, and biofuels. Palm oil goes by 200 different names, making it difficult to identify and even harder to determine sustainability.
